## 1. Battery Platforms Overview

Milwaukee operates three distinct battery platforms:

| Platform | Nominal Voltage | Max Voltage | Tool Count | Target Use |
|----------|----------------|-------------|------------|------------|
| **M12** | 12V | 12V | 100+ | Compact, precision, inspection, sub-compact tools |
| **M18** | 18V | 20V (no load) | 275+ (250+ tools) | Full-size power tools, most trades |
| **MX FUEL** | 72V | ~72V | 25+ | Light equipment replacing gas/corded (core drills, concrete saws, etc.) |

**Cross-compatibility rules:**
- All M18 batteries work with ALL M18 tools (backward and forward compatible)
- All M12 batteries work with ALL M12 tools (backward and forward compatible)
- M12 and M18 are NOT cross-compatible (different battery connections and voltage)
- MX FUEL is its own separate system
- Multi-voltage chargers can charge both M12 and M18 batteries

Source: [Acme Tools M12/M18 Compatibility Guide](https://www.acmetools.com/tool-crib/ac606579/ultimate-guide-to-milwaukee-m12-m18-compatibility/)

## 5. Battery Model Number Decoding

All Milwaukee batteries follow the format: **48-11-XXXX**

| Digits | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `48-11` | Milwaukee battery product identifier |
| `18XX` | M18 platform (18V) |
| `24XX` | M12 platform (12V) |

**Physical location of model numbers:** Front labels, battery base/nameplate labels, and original packaging near UPC code.

Source: [Genuine Tools Model Number Explainer](https://genuinetools.com/blogs/articles/milwaukee-battery-model-numbers-explained-code-location-series)
