## 1. Cordless Platform Overview

DeWalt operates four distinct cordless platforms, each targeting different use cases:

| Platform | Nominal Voltage | Marketing Voltage | Tool Count | Target User | Battery Chemistry |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 12V MAX | 10.8V | 12V | Dozens (30+) | Light DIY, tight-space work | Li-Ion |
| 20V MAX | 18V | 20V | 300+ products | DIY to professional | Li-Ion |
| 60V MAX | 54V | 60V | ~100 products | Heavy-duty professional | Li-Ion (FLEXVOLT) |
| POWERSHIFT | 554Wh | N/A | Limited (growing) | Industrial, gas-replacing | Li-Ion |

**Key note on voltage marketing:** DeWalt's "20V MAX" refers to the maximum initial battery voltage (off charger). The nominal operating voltage is 18V -- functionally identical to competitors' "18V" platforms (e.g., Makita 18V LXT, Milwaukee M18). This is a marketing convention, not a performance difference.

### Platform Cross-Compatibility Rules

| Battery Platform | Works in 12V Tools | Works in 20V Tools | Works in 60V Tools | Works in 120V Tools |
|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 12V MAX batteries | YES | NO | NO | NO |
| 20V MAX batteries | NO | YES | NO | NO |
| FLEXVOLT batteries | NO | YES (at 20V) | YES (at 60V) | YES (paired, at 120V) |
| 18V XRP (legacy) | NO | NO (adapter DCA1820 available) | NO | NO |

**Critical compatibility rules:**
- FLEXVOLT batteries are **backward compatible** with ALL 20V MAX tools (they run at 20V and provide extended runtime)
- Standard 20V MAX batteries **cannot** be used in 60V FLEXVOLT tools -- the tool slots have physical tabs preventing insertion
- The DCA1820 adapter allows 20V MAX batteries on legacy 18V tools, limited to 5.0Ah batteries; FLEXVOLT batteries cannot be used with this adapter
- 12V MAX and 20V MAX are completely separate, incompatible platforms

## 2. Product Line Hierarchy

DeWalt segments its 20V MAX tools into performance tiers. All tiers share the same battery platform.

### Tier Breakdown

| Tier | Motor Type | Target User | Size | Power Level | Price Point |
|------|-----------|-------------|------|-------------|-------------|
| **20V MAX** (base) | Brushed | Budget-conscious DIY, entry-level | Standard | Baseline | Lowest |
| **20V MAX ATOMIC** | Brushless | DIY, tight spaces, light pro | Compact (smallest) | Mid | Mid-low |
| **20V MAX XR** | Brushless | Professional, heavy use | Standard to full-size | High | Mid-high |
| **FLEXVOLT ADVANTAGE** | Brushless (enhanced) | Professional, power-hungry apps | Standard | Highest (with FV battery) | Highest |
| **FLEXVOLT 60V MAX** | Brushless | Heavy-duty pro, corded replacement | Full-size | Maximum | Premium |

### Detailed Tier Descriptions

#### 20V MAX (Base Line)
- Brushed motors (being phased out in newer models)
- Adequate for occasional DIY use
- Lower price of entry
- Compatible with all 20V MAX batteries

#### 20V MAX ATOMIC
- Brushless motors in the **most compact** form factor
- Designed for tight spaces and overhead work
- Lighter weight, reduced fatigue
- Lower power output compared to XR -- optimized for portability
- Example: DCF850 Impact Driver (less than 4 in. front-to-back, 1,825 in-lbs torque)

#### 20V MAX XR (eXtreme Runtime)
- Brushless motors with **maximum power and runtime**
- Professional-grade durability
- Longer battery life due to motor efficiency
- Higher torque and RPM ratings vs. ATOMIC
- Example: DCD800 Drill/Driver (900 UWO, 2,000 RPM)

#### FLEXVOLT ADVANTAGE
- 20V MAX tools with enhanced electronics that detect FLEXVOLT batteries
- When paired with a FLEXVOLT battery, delivers significantly more power than standard 20V
- Still works with regular 20V MAX batteries (at standard power)
- Available in: Circular Saw, Angle Grinder, Reciprocating Saw, Hammer Drill
- **Note:** Some reports indicate DeWalt may be phasing out Power Detect and FLEXVOLT Advantage branding in favor of new XR designations

#### FLEXVOLT 60V MAX
- Requires FLEXVOLT batteries (standard 20V batteries physically blocked)
- Designed to replace corded and gas-powered tools
- Table saws, large miter saws, grinders, rotary hammers, outdoor equipment
- Two FLEXVOLT batteries paired = 120V MAX for the most demanding tools

### Understanding DeWalt Model Numbers

DeWalt model numbers follow a pattern:
- **DCD** = Cordless Drill
  - DCD_X_0 to DCD_X_4 = Drill/Drivers
  - DCD_X_5 to DCD_X_9 = Hammer Drills
- **DCF** = Cordless Fastening (impact drivers, wrenches)
- **DCS** = Cordless Saw
- **DCG** = Cordless Grinder
- **DCN** = Cordless Nailer
- **DCH** = Cordless Hammer (rotary)
- **DCE** = Cordless Everything else (specialty)
- **DCB** = Battery/Charger
- **DW** = Corded or Accessory

**How FLEXVOLT voltage switching works:**
- Contains 15 internal lithium-ion cells
- Cells electronically reconfigure based on the connected tool
- In 20V MAX tools: cells in 5 groups of 3 (parallel) = ~20V nominal, higher Ah
- In 60V MAX tools: cells in 15 series = ~60V nominal, lower Ah
- Switching is automatic and instantaneous via slide connector detection

### Battery Technology Types

| Technology | Key Feature | Advantage | Cell Type |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Standard** | Traditional cylindrical cells | Proven, affordable | 18650 / 21700 |
| **POWERSTACK** | Pouch cell technology | 50% more power, 25% more compact, 15% lighter, 2x lifespan | Flat pouch |
| **POWERPACK** | Tabless cylindrical cells | Optimized for longer runtimes | Tabless cylindrical |
| **FLEXVOLT** | Dual-voltage switching | One battery for 20V and 60V tools | 15-cell reconfigurable |

### Battery Charging Temperature

- Optimal charging range: 40-104 degrees F (4-40 degrees C)
- Hot batteries should cool before charging to prevent cell stress
- All 20V MAX and FLEXVOLT batteries require compatible DeWalt chargers with internal battery management system communication

### Key DeWalt Competitive Advantages
1. **FLEXVOLT dual-voltage system** -- unique in the industry; one battery for 20V and 60V tools

3. **ATOMIC compact line** -- compact brushless tools at lower price than competitors' compact lines

1. **Smaller tool count** than Makita LXT and Milwaukee M18
2. **Voltage marketing confusion** -- "20V MAX" vs competitors' "18V" confuses consumers
3. **FLEXVOLT required for table/miter saws** -- adds battery cost for saw users
4. **Home Depot/Lowe's split** -- FLEXVOLT only at Home Depot limits availability
